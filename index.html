About This Research

This work package is part of the ENTRUST Doctoral Network, a Horizon Europe Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions (MSCA) project bringing together Early Stage Researchers across European institutions to address critical challenges in data governance for agricultural ecosystems.

Agricultural data sharing presents unique governance challenges: farmers own their data but lack the infrastructure and legal frameworks to fully exercise that ownership. Our research develops formal, implementable solutions grounded in three complementary and interconnected pillars — sovereignty, trust, and security.

Together, the three pillars form a complete governance layer: data cannot be shared responsibly without enforcing ownership rights, verifying the trustworthiness of all parties, and securing the channel and content of every exchange.

Research Areas

Three Research Pillars

Each pillar addresses a distinct but interconnected dimension of data governance in agricultural ecosystems. Together they form a complete, formally grounded governance framework.

🔒
Pillar 1

Data Sovereignty

Problem

Agricultural data flows from IoT sensors, tractors, and drones directly into platforms controlled by agri-tech companies. By the time a farmer realises their data has been shared, aggregated, or sold, there is nothing technically stopping it. Existing legal frameworks like the EU Data Act deliberately avoid defining any clear rights over this data, so governance defaults to contracts — EULAs and data-sharing agreements that are typically long, written at university reading level, and drafted entirely by the technology provider.

Objective

This research aims to design a sovereign data sharing platform for agriculture where usage control policies are digitally enforced, not merely written in a contract. The platform architecture itself becomes the governance mechanism.

Pillar 2

Trust & Trustworthiness

Develops formal frameworks for evaluating and communicating the trustworthiness of actors and systems in agricultural data sharing networks. Research addresses how farmers can make informed, evidence-based decisions about who to share their data with and under what conditions — grounded in established trust theory and modern verifiable credential standards.

Pillar 3

Cybersecurity

Examines threat models specific to agricultural data ecosystems and develops security primitives that protect the integrity, confidentiality, and availability of agricultural data throughout the sharing lifecycle — from IoT field sensor to analytical platform. Applies the STRIDE methodology adapted to the constraints of resource-limited agricultural infrastructure.

State of the Art

Key works shaping the research landscape across our three pillars, with identified gaps that our work package addresses.

Reference Year Pillar Key Contribution Gap Addressed by Our Work
Michels et al.
Governing Agricultural Data 		2020 Sovereignty Identifies structural barriers to farmer data ownership in digital agriculture platforms No formal enforcement mechanism proposed
Wiseman et al.
Farmers and their Data 		2019 Sovereignty Empirical study of farmer attitudes toward data sharing and ownership in the US Descriptive only; no governance or enforcement framework
IDSA
Reference Architecture Model v4 		2022 Sovereignty Standardised connector architecture for sovereign industrial data spaces Too infrastructurally heavy for small-farm contexts; no DP support
EU Regulation 2016/679
General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) 		2016 Sovereignty Legal basis for data subject rights, consent, and processing accountability Legal instrument only; not technically enforceable at system level
Dwork et al.
Calibrating Noise to Sensitivity in Private Data Analysis 		2006 Sovereignty Foundational differential privacy framework — the Laplace mechanism Not applied to agricultural export contexts or sovereignty architectures
Van Kleek & O'Hara
Why Re-decentralizing the Web? 		2019 Sovereignty Argues for returning data control to individuals via decentralised architectures Agricultural domain specifics and enforcement mechanisms not addressed
Mayer, Davis & Schoorman
An Integrative Model of Organizational Trust 		1995 Trust Seminal model: ability, benevolence, and integrity as antecedents of trust Not operationalised for digital agricultural data sharing systems
Artz & Gil
A Survey of Trust in Computer Science and the Semantic Web 		2007 Trust Comprehensive taxonomy of computational trust models Pre-dates decentralised identity, verifiable credentials, and agricultural IoT
W3C
Verifiable Credentials Data Model 2.0 		2023 Trust Standard for cryptographically verifiable digital credentials No domain-specific agricultural trust profiles or scoring models
Hardin
Trust and Trustworthiness 		2002 Trust Encapsulated interest account; key distinction between trust and trustworthiness Philosophical framework; lacks a computational or measurable model
Gaia-X AISBL
Gaia-X Trust Framework 		2023 Trust European federated trust framework for cloud and data infrastructure Agriculture-specific trust requirements and actor profiles not addressed
Grandison & Sloman
A Survey of Trust in Internet Applications 		2000 Trust Foundational survey of trust in distributed internet systems Predates modern data ecosystems, IoT, and decentralised identifiers
ENISA
Cybersecurity in the Agriculture Sector 		2021 Cybersecurity First EU-level threat landscape assessment for the agricultural sector Descriptive only; no technical countermeasures or formal threat models
Rose et al. (NIST)
SP 800-207: Zero Trust Architecture 		2020 Cybersecurity Foundational zero-trust security model replacing perimeter-based defences Not adapted for resource-constrained agricultural IoT environments
Kamilaris et al.
A Review on the Practice of Big Data Analysis in Agriculture 		2017 Cybersecurity Identifies security as an open problem in agricultural big data pipelines Security concerns are raised but not systematically addressed
Frustaci et al.
Evaluating Critical Security Issues of the IoT 		2018 Cybersecurity Systematic analysis of IoT security vulnerabilities and attack surfaces Agricultural IoT specifics, data governance integration not covered
Shostack
Threat Modeling: Designing for Security 		2014 Cybersecurity STRIDE methodology for systematic threat identification in system design Not applied to agricultural data sharing architectures

A domain-agnostic Python library for sovereignty invariant enforcement. Implements cryptographic policy binding, signed export manifests, hash-chained audit logs, differential privacy (Laplace), and LSB steganographic watermarking.
